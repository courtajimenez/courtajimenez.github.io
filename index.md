Courtney A. Jimenez, Ph.D., is a research scientist working at the intersection of computational neuroscience and reinforcement learning. She recently completed her Ph.D. in Psychology (Social Neuroscience) at Columbia University, where she conducted research in the [Columbia Social Neuroscience Lab](http://columbiasocialneurosciencelab.com/) with [Dr. Meghan Meyer](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=4tyQoi0AAAAJ&hl=en), studying the neural bases of social learning and memory using high-dimensional fMRI neuroimaging analysis and computational modeling.

Courtney's current work focuses on Social Cognition - applying neuroimaging and reinforcement learning frameworks to model and understand social learning, memory, and decision-making, implemented in Python and R. She is actively seeking data scientist roles that bridge human behavior and machine learning, with particular interest in organizations advancing computational models of human cognition and behavior.

Courtney previously received an M.A. in Psychological and Brain Sciences from [Dartmouth College](https://pbs.dartmouth.edu/), and a B.S. in Neurobiology, Physiology, and Behavior and a B.A. in Psychology from the [University of California, Davis](https://www.ucdavis.edu/).
